Our Auto Painting Process

Meticulous preparation and application for flawless results

1

Initial Consultation & Estimate

We begin with a thorough assessment of your vehicle and discuss your goals, color preferences, and budget. Our color specialists help you select the perfect finish and provide a detailed estimate.

2

Surface Preparation

Proper preparation is critical for a quality paint job. We carefully clean, sand, and repair the surface, removing old paint, rust, dents, and imperfections to create a smooth foundation for the new paint.

3

Masking & Protection

We meticulously mask and protect all areas that should not receive paint, including windows, trim, and mechanical components, ensuring clean lines and preventing overspray.

4

Primer Application

A high-quality primer is applied to create a uniform surface, enhance paint adhesion, and provide additional corrosion protection. The primed surface is then carefully sanded to ensure smoothness.

5

Base Coat Application

The color coat is applied in our state-of-the-art paint booth under controlled conditions to ensure a dust-free environment and optimal temperature and humidity for perfect application.

6

Clear Coat Application

A protective clear coat is applied over the base color to provide depth, gloss, and long-term protection against UV rays, oxidation, and environmental damage.

7

Curing & Finishing

The paint is properly cured using our advanced systems. Once cured, we perform color sanding and buffing as needed to remove any minor imperfections and maximize gloss and smoothness.

8

Final Inspection & Delivery

Our quality control team inspects the finished paint job under various lighting conditions to ensure perfection. We then clean and detail your vehicle before returning it to you with care instructions for your new finish.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our auto painting services

How long does an auto painting job take?

The timeline for an auto painting job depends on several factors, including the extent of preparation needed, the type of paint being applied, and whether any body work is required. A standard complete vehicle painting typically takes 5-7 business days. Spot repairs and panel painting may take 2-3 days. Custom paint jobs with special finishes or multiple colors can take 2-3 weeks. We'll provide you with a specific timeline estimate during your consultation.

How much does it cost to paint a car?

Auto painting costs vary based on several factors: vehicle size, current condition, amount of preparation required, paint type selected, and whether any body repairs are needed. Basic solid color paint jobs start around $1,200 for an average-sized vehicle, while premium finishes like pearls, metallics, and custom colors range from $1,800 to $3,500+. Partial painting and spot repairs are priced based on the specific areas being painted. We provide detailed, transparent estimates after inspecting your vehicle.

How long will my new paint job last?

With proper care, a professional paint job from Auto Body Shop Philadelphia should last 10-15 years. We use premium automotive paints with UV-resistant clear coats that provide excellent durability. Factors affecting longevity include exposure to harsh weather conditions, whether the vehicle is garaged, and maintenance practices. We provide a lifetime warranty against defects in workmanship for as long as you own the vehicle. Regular washing, waxing, and avoiding harsh chemicals will help maintain your paint's appearance and longevity.

Can you match my vehicle's original color exactly?

Yes, we use computerized color matching technology to precisely match your vehicle's original color. Every vehicle has a color code that we can reference, and our mixing system can recreate factory colors with exceptional accuracy. For older vehicles where the paint may have faded or oxidized over time, we can adjust the formula to match the current appearance or restore it to the original factory color. Our paint specialists are experts at color matching and will ensure a seamless blend between new and existing paint.

Do I need to prepare my vehicle before bringing it in for painting?

While we handle all technical preparation as part of our service, there are a few things you can do before bringing your vehicle in: remove personal belongings from the interior, remove any external accessories you've added (like antennas or custom emblems), and ensure the vehicle is reasonably clean. If you're having only specific panels painted, it helps to wash the vehicle so we can better assess the color match. Our team will handle all surface preparation, including cleaning, sanding, masking, and priming.

Are your automotive paints environmentally friendly?

Yes, we use modern waterborne and low-VOC (Volatile Organic Compounds) paints that comply with environmental regulations while providing excellent durability and appearance. These paints release fewer harmful emissions during application and curing compared to traditional solvent-based paints. Our paint booth features advanced filtration systems to capture overspray and minimize environmental impact. We also properly dispose of all paint-related waste according to EPA guidelines.
